I had to make an ethical decision of moving away from my parents and living by myself in New Zealand. This might sound easy for some people, however it is not an easy move for an only child in a Chinese family. As the traditional Chinese culture, children are responsible for taking care of their parents when they get old. Therefore most of the Chinese people will choose to stay close with their parents, ie. living in the same city. However, I believe that being independent and having the courage to pursue my dream are the key values in becoming success.

I had been welly protected by my family since I was born, I did not need to worry about anything as everything was prepared for me. When I first moved out, I stuggled a lot. I thought that 24 hours for a day was never enough; I had to work, to study and to take care all the bits and pieces of myself, challenges were everywhere. However, I have never douted the decision I made, because challenges are opportunities for growth. After all these years, I have learned how to plan and prioritise different tasks, how to balance work and personal life, how to take care of myself as well as others. This is a life-long lesson and I am pursuing a better vesion of myself everyday.

Share an example from your experience of where you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss the strategies you used at that time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now, and why.

Previously I worked as a mentor in my team and was responsible for training new people, making sure they are familiar with our company workflow and systems. The newbies came from different backgrounds and had different approach on learning new things, hence there were a large difference on their learning progress. Some people learned much faster than the others. People came asking me same questions at different times, I had to stop and explain the same concept again and again.

I realized that I had to find a way to improve the training efficiency. I created a trello page (an online tasks management tool), shared the FAQs on the page, so that I could direct people to the page to find the answers themselves. In that way, I did not need to slow down the training progress of the whole team, at the same time people can find answers in their own time according to their learning progress. I have been using the same technique for my daily tasks to share information with my team mates.

I would say that was not the best approach for a training situation, because some people might look for the answers straight away before trying to find the answers by themselves. It I have a chance to be in that situation again, I would take more collaborative approaches, for example creating a SLACK platform and let people helping each other.
